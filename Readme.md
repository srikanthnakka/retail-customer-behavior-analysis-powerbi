# Retail Customer Behavior Analysis (Power BI)

## Project Overview

Understanding customer behavior is critical for improving revenue growth, customer loyalty, and marketing effectiveness. Retail businesses generate large amounts of transactional data that can reveal purchasing patterns, customer segments, and retention risks.

This project analyzes retail transaction data to understand **customer purchasing behavior, revenue drivers, and loyalty patterns** using **Power BI dashboards**.

The analysis focuses on identifying high-value customers, evaluating the effectiveness of discount strategies, and uncovering opportunities for improving customer retention.

| Metric               | Value  |

| Average Order Value  | $59.76 |

These metrics provide a high-level view of overall customer engagement and revenue performance.

# Business Problem

Retail companies must understand customer purchasing patterns to improve retention and optimize promotional strategies.

However, businesses often struggle to answer questions such as:

* Which customer segments generate the most revenue?
* Do discounts actually improve customer loyalty?
* Which age groups contribute the most to revenue?
* Which sales channels perform best?
* Which customers are at risk of churn?

This project uses **interactive dashboards** to answer these questions and support data-driven decision-making.

# Customer Age Segment Analysis

Customer age segmentation reveals differences in purchasing behavior.

| Age Group | Revenue Contribution   |
| --------- | ---------------------- |
| 26–35     | Highest revenue        |
| 36–45     | Moderate revenue       |
| 46+       | High loyalty potential |
| 18–25     | Lower spending         |

The **26–35 segment drives the largest revenue**, while the **46+ segment shows stronger repeat purchasing behavior**.

---

# Discount Impact Analysis

One key goal of the analysis was to evaluate the effect of discounts on customer loyalty.

Key findings:

* Discounts slightly improve repeat purchase rate
* Discount usage does **not significantly increase average order value**
* Non-discounted transactions generate comparable revenue

This suggests that **broad discount strategies may not significantly improve long-term retention**.

# Key Insights

Several important insights were discovered during the analysis.

### Discounts have limited impact on revenue

Discounts do not significantly increase average order value or customer loyalty.

### Online sales dominate revenue

Online transactions account for the majority of total sales.

### Customer age influences spending behavior

The 26–35 age group drives the most revenue, while the 46+ group demonstrates strong loyalty potential.

### High-value customers drive revenue

Although smaller in number, high-value customers generate significant revenue and should be prioritized in retention strategies.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the insights from the dashboard, several strategic recommendations can be made.

### Focus on High-Value Customers

Implement loyalty programs and personalized marketing campaigns to retain high-value customers.

### Optimize Promotional Strategy

Rather than broad discounting, retailers should adopt targeted promotions based on customer behavior.

### Improve Customer Retention

Engagement campaigns should focus on customers identified as being at risk of churn.

### Leverage Online Sales Channels

Investing further in digital channels can help maximize revenue growth.
